Connect 4.0

Physical Computing

Gizmo | December 2021

Connect 4.0 combines the tactile interaction of traditional board games with the smart and responsive experience that we're all used to with mobile gaming. The LCD display prompts each player to make their moves whilst the infrared sensors on the top of each column will keep track of where each player has gone. An Arduino mega board on the back of the game is used to process these inputs and lets you know when the game has been won. The board is made from 4mm plywood board that was laser cut in the workshop. Finger joints are used to make the structure more durable. The counters are made from 3D-printed PLA which was spray painted neon pink and green. This colour scheme was carried through to the project poster to achieve a bright and playful design. I was able to display this project as part of the ‘Design Futures’ exhibit at the Great Exhibition Road Festival in June 2022. It was very gratifying to see so many visitors playing with it, and it served as a great opportubity for large-scale user testing.
